## Key Decisions
Bottom-up cost modeling identifies two pathways for gold recovery at 0.8-3.2 ppb influent. The SLUDGE ROUTE is preferred: at these influent concentrations, sludge gold (1.9-7.5 mg/kg) reaches conventional mining ore grade, with estimated extraction cost of $27-109/g depending on concentration — economic at ≥1.2 ppb with $33-115/g margin at current gold prices ($142.4/g). The DISSOLVED ROUTE is inferior ($108-431/g), breaking even only at ≥2.8 ppb, because it must process 667x more volume for 30% of the gold. No published TEA validates these estimates; three critical unknowns remain: thiourea consumption in organic sludge, actual gold partitioning to sludge, and sorbent Au(I) compatibility at ppb.
