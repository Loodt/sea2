## Key Decisions
Optimal CCD washing for IX-RIL tailings to achieve <10 mg/kg residual CN: PRIMARY recommendation is 4 stages of high-density thickeners at 55% underflow solids, wash ratio 2.5:1, using fresh water — yielding ~5.4 mg/kg residual and 97.8% CN recovery to closed loop. Conservative fallback is 5 stages with high-rate thickeners at 45% UF. The <10 mg/kg target is 3-10x stricter than regulations (product-quality driven for geopolymer), making bench-scale settling tests on Wits slimes critical for confirming achievable UF density. Stage efficiency degrades significantly past stage 4, supporting the 4-stage HD design over aggressive 3-stage configurations. WPS complements CCD by stripping CN from overflow and returning near-zero-CN water as wash feed, closing the water-cyanide loop. Annual NaCN savings ~$10M at 6 Mt/yr throughput.
