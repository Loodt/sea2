## Key Decisions
Combined ash+dissolved recovery outperforms either standalone at intermediate partitioning (40-60%) for plants with existing sludge incineration, generating 29-87% more annual profit at 1.6 ppb. The ash route (F1040) is 3-6x cheaper per gram than the killed raw-sludge model, with $21-47/g costs. The combined strategy's cost/g is remarkably insensitive to partitioning (~$51-52/g), making it a natural hedge against speciation uncertainty. The exception is at very low concentration (0.8 ppb, p≥50%) where dissolved infrastructure is uneconomic and ash-only wins. Critical constraint: only 14% of US WWTPs have existing incineration, limiting the combined strategy's addressable market.
