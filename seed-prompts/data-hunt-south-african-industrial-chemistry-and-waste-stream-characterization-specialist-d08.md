## Key Decisions
Q011 is answered with high confidence across all four sub-questions. SACC Newcastle generates carbide lime sludge on-site from acetylene production -- a high-purity Ca(OH)2 stream (85-95% portlandite, pH 12.2) with manageable heavy metal and sulfide/phosphide contaminants that are effectively immobilized in geopolymer matrices. The sludge is generated as wet slurry (65-90% water) but can be dewatered to 15% moisture via membrane filtration or used partially dried when blended with dry precursors. Physical properties (d50 9-27 um, BET 7.05 m2/g) are favorable for direct use without grinding. The practical access path centers on SACC Ballengeich (~42,000 t/yr theoretical dry Ca(OH)2), within 300 km of PG and fly ash sources, with the sludge being a disposal liability that creates commercial incentive for off-take.
