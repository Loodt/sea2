## Key Decisions
This iteration converted the earlier fairness intuition into an explicit measurement contract for realized developmental attention quality: usable opportunity, mediation fit, repair responsiveness, continuity, and parent alignment. From that contract, the architecture consequence is clear: Jarvis must store and act on per-child attention gaps and unmet obligations, not just retrospective memory. The main design shift is from symmetric provision to asymmetric orchestration, including unequal cadence, escalation, follow-up, and parent-coaching intensity by need. Under that frame, provision metrics and neutral treatment of connectivity/caregiver asymmetry break, while one-Jarvis continuity, persistent memory, and parent coaching survive only if redesigned as compensating mechanisms.
