## Key Decisions
Three-stage investigation concludes NEGATIVE on the de minimis safe-harbour hypothesis. Stage 1-2 (instrument layer, iter 2) found no express carve-out in GN 1350/2022, the General Exemption, or the FSCA Policy Document. Stage 3 (jurisprudence + Ombud practice, iter 3) confirmed (a) TriStar SCA does not create the safe harbour and (b) FAIS Ombud reads 'regular feature' functionally — isolated transactions embedded in ongoing service relationships fall within jurisdiction. Closed-membership savings-club status provides no FAIS scope-out, and a single primary distribution event organised as the club's central activity is itself the regular feature of that vehicle's business. Au-Token Class A distribution must therefore route through a Cat I CASP, secure a FSCA s44 individual exemption, or be re-characterised outside the crypto-asset / financial-product definition.
