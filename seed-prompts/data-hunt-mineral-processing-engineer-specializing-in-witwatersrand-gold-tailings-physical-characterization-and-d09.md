## Key Decisions
The Witwatersrand slime:sand split is determined primarily by processing era, not basin geography. Pre-1950 stamp-milled sand dumps are 80-90% sand (>63µm); post-1950 ball-milled slimes dams are 70-95% slime (<74µm). Since peak gold production was post-1950, the majority of the basin's ~6 Bt inventory is slime-dominant. Within individual TSFs, hydraulic segregation creates sandy beaches near discharge points and slimy centers. No published basin-wide tonnage split exists, but the Crown Mines example (50 Mt sand : 20 Mt slime) represents early-era conditions only. For the total-value-recovery architecture, hydrocyclone cut point selection will determine the operational split regardless of feed composition.
