## Key Decisions
The SA digital freight landscape stratifies into four tiers: (1) Linebooker dominates as a managed broker-marketplace that guarantees carrier payment and serves JSE Top 40 shippers — highest switching cost but structurally not neutral; (2) MyLoad is the most architecturally interesting competitor with blind bidding and escrow but lacks forward-matching, settlement acceleration, and governance for neutrality; (3) Apexloads, Saloodo, Bid Logistics, and BidShip offer varying thin marketplace layers with limited SA long-haul traction; (4) Transhub, Loads.co.za, RFA lists, and WhatsApp groups are passive/informal channels with zero structural depth. No existing platform combines structural neutrality, blind capacity matching, settlement acceleration, and hard-constraint enforcement — the proposed exchange occupies an uncontested structural position. The primary competitive barrier is Linebooker's payment guarantee, which the exchange's settlement rail must match.
