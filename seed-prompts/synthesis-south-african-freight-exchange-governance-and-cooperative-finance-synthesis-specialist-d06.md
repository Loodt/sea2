## Key Decisions
The fast-kill screen compared three branches: VC marketplace, pure participant-owned cooperative utility, and an independent neutral operator with separated finance. The VC branch fails because payment improvement comes from intermediary control of funds and financing, which recreates broker behavior and capital dependence. The pure cooperative branch is safer but still exposes the exchange to incumbent capture because South African freight is anchored by large hybrid operators and participant-owned utilities commonly embed concentrated control rights. The strongest surviving structure is a neutral operator utility with non-distributing governance, independent-majority oversight, cost-recovery operating fees, and an optional early-pay facility that is contractually and balance-sheet separated from the exchange itself.
