## Key Decisions
Witwatersrand gold tailings are quartz-dominated (58-82%) aluminosilicate materials with Si:Al molar ratios of 3.6-17:1 in bulk — too silica-rich for direct geopolymer use. However, simple size fractionation at 63 µm splits the material into (a) high-purity silica sand (SiO2 ~89%, Si:Al ~17:1) suitable for glass/industrial silica, and (b) clay-rich slimes (~45% clay minerals, estimated Si:Al ~1.5-2.5:1) within geopolymer precursor specification. Significant composition variability exists across the basin: West Rand dumps are more aluminous (Al2O3 10-15%) while East Rand material is extremely silica-rich (SiO2 ~89%). Six alumina-bearing mineral phases identified — all concentrated in the slime fraction and geopolymer-reactive.
