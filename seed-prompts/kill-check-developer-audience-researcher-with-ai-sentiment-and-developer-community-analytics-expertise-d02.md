## Key Decisions
Developers react to AI-generated marketing content differently from consumers in kind, not just magnitude, across five categorical dimensions: detection mechanism (technical markers vs emotional cues), enforcement (institutional bans/forks vs individual disengagement), framing (accuracy/trust violation vs authenticity concern), response speed (days vs weeks), and verification behavior (adopt-and-verify vs suspect-and-avoid). Developer distrust runs 7-19 percentage points higher than consumer distrust, with only 3.1% of developers highly trusting AI outputs. The B2B trust gap is even more extreme: 4% of B2B marketers report high AI content trust vs 55% of marketers generally. For HERALD, this means any AI-generated content targeting developers must pass technical scrutiny at the implementation detail level, and community-level enforcement mechanisms pose a risk beyond individual user rejection.
