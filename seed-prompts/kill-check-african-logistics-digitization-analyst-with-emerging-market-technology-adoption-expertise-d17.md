## Key Decisions
Seven categories of barriers have killed or stalled freight digitization in Sub-Saharan Africa. The dominant killer — working-capital trap from intermediating carrier payment (Kobo360, Lori, Sendy) — is structurally eliminated by the proposed exchange's funder-controlled settlement design. Three pan-African infrastructure barriers (connectivity, digital literacy, cash culture) do not bind on the SA formal-fleet pilot. Of four SA-specific adoption barriers that do bind, three are fully mitigated by architecture (integration burden via build-internally; competitive intelligence fear via blind matching; relationship inertia via backhaul-only targeting) and one is partially mitigated (margin compression via cost-recovery + settlement acceleration, but fee tolerance is empirically unverified). All residual risks are commercial/empirical, not architectural — the design is sound against every identified structural barrier.
