## Key Decisions
Q007 is answered: coal ash + sulfidic tailings constitutes a legitimate water-activated material class in the barrier/backfill category (1-3 MPa), not structural geopolymer (20+ MPa). Field evidence from Musselwhite mine proves water-only cementation works at scale. Performance exceeds barrier minimums (0.1-1 MPa) by 3-10x. Waste-only binder combinations (CKD+fly ash) meet EPA solidification/stabilization standards on highly sulfidic tailings. Natural hardpan cements persist 44+ years. For South Africa, 500+ Mt of coal ash stockpile and extensive sulfidic tailings from gold, coal, and base metal mining make this a viable mine rehabilitation product class — categorized as 'stabilized mine residue' or 'cemented paste fill,' not geopolymer.
