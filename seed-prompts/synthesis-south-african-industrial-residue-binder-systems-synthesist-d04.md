## Key Decisions
Stage 1 is sufficient to close Q001 as a bounded role-map question. Coal ash is the only strong South African dump-scale aluminosilicate precursor class still alive, GGBFS is the cleanest latent-calcium source, phosphogypsum is the sulfate/mineralizer leg, and siliceous mine tailings are filler rather than binder chemistry. Internal alkalinity is the narrowest function: the strongest surviving South African candidate is carbide-derived lime/sludge from the Newcastle acetylene chain, while waste glass fines remain only a conditional alkali/silica helper. The main negative results are part of the answer: generic tailings do not supply the reactive binder chemistry, generic BOF slag is not a straight ladle-slag substitute, and phosphogypsum should not be over-credited beyond the sulfate/mineralizer role.
