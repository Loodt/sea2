## Key Decisions
The surface-plant South African route is not "just IECEx paperwork": SABS states Ex equipment used on surface in South Africa should be certified and issued with IA certificates, so a hazardous-area SKU needs a real certification pathway rather than a casual self-declaration [SOURCE: https://www.sabs.co.za/explosion-prevention-technology-rotating-machines]. For the gold-processing hazards evidenced so far, the rational target is localized Zone 2 gas and localized Zone 22 dust, with a standards stack centered on SANS/IEC 60079-0 plus 60079-11 for gas-side intrinsic safety and optionally 60079-31 where dust protection by enclosure is claimed [DERIVED: F939; SOURCE: https://webstore.iec.ch/en/publication/60654; SOURCE: https://webstore.iec.ch/en/publication/32540]. IECEx repeat-production approval requires both sample testing and a factory QAR with surveillance, which is why the timeline burden is months rather than weeks and why unit verification is a poor fit for sticker-scale products [SOURCE: https://www.iecex.com/certified-equipment-scheme/overview/]. The practical implication is economic triage: safe-area first is the easiest launch path, Zone 2-only or Zone 22-only is the plausible first hazardous-area SKU, and a combined 2/22 or ATEX-export variant should be treated as materially slower and more expensive, with first-pass budgets in the tens-of-thousands of USD rather than low-cost consumer-electronics certification spend [ESTIMATED: F941].
