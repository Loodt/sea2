## Key Decisions
Bushveld chrome/PGM tailings are now comprehensively characterised across UG2, Merensky, and MG chromitite types. UG2 tailings are chromite-rich (10-46 wt%, Cr2O3 15-34%) with silicate gangue (OPX, plagioclase) and very low BMS (0.03 wt%); PGMs are silicate-locked (57%) at 0.7-1.0 g/t 4E with 8-9 µm grain size. Merensky tailings are silicate-dominated (OPX 30-60%, plag 20-40%) with chromite only in thin stringers, but carry 10-50× more BMS (0.3-1.6 wt%) where PGMs are BMS-associated at 15-20 µm grain size. MG chromitite tailings have the finest PGMs (2-3 µm) but highest liberation (66.5%). Iron is carried primarily by chromite spinel (FeO 26%), with ferrichromite rims (FeO 55-67%) forming on weathered surfaces. The industry holds ~450 Mt of accumulated tailings with PGM grades of 0.7-1.6 g/t.
