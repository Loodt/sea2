## Key Decisions
[SOURCE] Bushveld chrome-recovery tailings are not leaving behind a discrete quartz or iron-oxide residue; the dominant remaining gangue is a silicate assemblage centered on orthopyroxene, plagioclase, and clinopyroxene, with some residual fine chromite. [SOURCE] The unrecovered valuable/minor phases are mainly fine liberated chromite plus ultrafine silicate-locked BMS/PGE composites, which explains why slimes/final tails remain mineralogically mixed. [SOURCE] Demonstrated secondary value is narrow: coarse fractions have documented internal civil use in TSF drainage/filter construction, and mixed PGM tailings have demonstrated chemical extractability of Al, Fe, Si, Ca, Mg, and Cr. [DERIVED] For the specific categories asked, construction use is demonstrated only internally, while alumina-bearing feeds, iron units, and silica products remain plausible but not evidenced as commercial post-chrome Bushveld products.
