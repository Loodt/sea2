## Key Decisions
Q008 is answered with 12+ distinct no-external-activator binder formulas catalogued across 4 mechanism classes (supersulfated with waste catalysts, waste-alkali, true self-activation, classic SSC with minor clinker). The highest-confidence SA translation is the F937 family: 35% anhydrous phosphogypsum + 60% GGBFS + 5% carbide slag (51.4 MPa, fully clinker-free). All three components exist in the SA supply chain — GGBFS from AfriSam Vaal, phosphogypsum from Phalaborwa, carbide lime from Newcastle. Critical kill: South African ferrochrome slag (the largest slag stream by volume) fails SSC chemistry requirements due to very low CaO, narrowing the viable SA geography to the Vaal Triangle and Newcastle corridor. The remaining uncertainties are empirical (SA GGBFS sulfate content, PG impurity profile) and require lab testing, not further literature search.
