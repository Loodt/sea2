## Key Decisions
The buyer journey for agentic software is structurally different across three segments. Developers discover through peers/social platforms, evaluate by trying quickstarts and checking docs, and decide in days based on maintenance signals and setup friction. Technical founders discover through communities (Reddit, Indie Hackers, X), evaluate through build-vs-buy analysis, and decide in weeks based on integration fit and vendor viability. Enterprise buyers discover through analysts and vendor outreach, evaluate through multi-stakeholder committees running POCs, and decide in months based on governance, security compliance, and measured ROI. Trust signals shift from 'does it work for me personally?' (developer) to 'can I defend this purchase to my committee?' (enterprise). Five universal evaluation criteria emerged from G2 data: data integration depth, governance/auditability, orchestration readiness, human-in-the-loop design, and speed-to-value.
