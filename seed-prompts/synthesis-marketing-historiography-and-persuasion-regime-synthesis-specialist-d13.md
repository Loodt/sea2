## Key Decisions
The regime model survives the evidence-bias cautions, but not as five equally hard historical breaks. The strongest real shifts are changes in the dominant persuasion burden: social permission to choice legibility, then to hidden-quality trust shortcuts, and in some categories to participatory circulation. The platform-branding to participation boundary and the 2020s trust-fragmentation story are both real but mixed with disclosure effects. The biggest artifact is the apparent late-century rise in marketing 'science' and explicit strategy, which is strongly amplified by modern case-study and measurement systems.
