## Key Decisions
Iteration 3 closed the last live mechanism for Q014. A 2026 quartz-hematite tailings analogue confirms that 800 °C for 10 minutes can create thermoelastic microcracks and improve post-roast magnetic separation, so the liberation-only mechanism is real in principle. But the Wits-specific case still does not favor roasting: prior iterations already showed that an oxidizing short roast mainly converts secondary Wits iron phases to hematite rather than to stronger magnetic phases, while the existing Wits-relevant direct-WHIMS baseline already plausibly reaches sub-0.5 wt.% Fe2O3 without acid. The new thermodynamic and tariff screen shows the roast adds a minimum dry-solid penalty of about 0.83 GJ/t or 230 kWh/t, equivalent to roughly R225-R705/t before furnace losses, which is large relative to direct WHIMS field energy. Therefore roast plus WHIMS is not the justified no-acid baseline for Wits tailings sand; direct WHIMS remains the credible route unless bench data later shows a major roast-only liberation gain.
