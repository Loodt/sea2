## Key Decisions
A concrete pilot term-sheet structure exists for advancing against approved delivered backhaul invoices on JHB-CPT/JHB-DBN without pulling the exchange into principal credit risk. The structure is a disclosed approved-invoice purchase or debtor-finance facility where the carrier assigns exchange-verified delivered invoices to an external funder (bank, factor, or fintech) that advances 70-90%, holds a 20-30% reserve, controls the collection account, and bears the credit decision on shipper-debtors. The exchange is limited to POD verification, invoice-status data, and reconciliation support — it never takes cession, guarantees payment, or custodies funds. Six publicly identifiable counterparty categories fit: Absa selective receivable financing (best for narrow blue-chip debtor pilot), Standard Bank debtor finance (established carriers), FNB selective invoice discounting and Nedbank Debtor Management (alternatives), Merchant Factors (SME carriers, 15-20yr transport track record), and Thumeza (micro-carriers via embedded finance). Optional credit-insurance wraps from CGIC or Allianz Trade SA (up to 95% indemnity) further de-risk the funder. The NCA regulatory boundary is non-blocking for B2B juristic persons above R1M. Five failure modes were stress-tested; all are manageable at pilot scale. Remaining unknowns require direct funder engagement rather than further public research.
