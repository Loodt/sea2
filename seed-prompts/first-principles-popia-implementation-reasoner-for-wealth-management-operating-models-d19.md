## Key Decisions
I continued from F926-F929 rather than restarting and stress-tested the package against the key factual split the prior iteration identified: necessary specialist continuity review versus optional enrichment review. That split holds: mandatory continuity capture and genuinely necessary internal specialist support can ride on a non-consent servicing/compatibility lane if the purpose is specified up front, while optional enrichment needs either a narrow legitimate-interests lane with objection or, more cleanly, separate opt-in consent. I then tightened that into an executable notice package with client-facing mandatory continuity wording, a distinct objection path for any legitimate-interests review, separate opt-in wording for optional enrichment, and adviser-facing internal capture rules. Q024 is therefore answerable at the operating-model level, with three explicit escalation boundaries: special personal information, specialist review that is not actually necessary for servicing, and any multi-responsible-party structure in practice.
