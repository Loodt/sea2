## Key Decisions
Municipal sewage contains Cu, Zn, Fe, DOC, sulfide, and chloride at 10^3-10^5x gold concentration. Three proven selectivity mechanisms handle metal interference: HSAB soft-soft selectivity (10^3-10^6 separation factors), redox selectivity (Au uniquely reducible), and size-selective pore exclusion in MOFs/COFs. Interference severity ranks DOC/NOM > sulfide > Cu > other metals > chloride. DOC remains the critical unvalidated gap — no sorbent tested in real sewage DOC at ppb gold. Piggyback oxidative pretreatment synergistically destroys sulfide and DOC while liberating thiolate-bound gold, making it the recommended first step before sorbent capture.
