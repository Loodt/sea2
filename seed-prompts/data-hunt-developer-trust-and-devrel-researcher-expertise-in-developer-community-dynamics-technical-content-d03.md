## Key Decisions
QQ005 answered with three-layer evidence convergence. The unifying mechanism for developer AI trust is VERIFIABILITY — developers trust what they can personally verify. Proof-type effectiveness ranking: (1) hands-on trial/PLG, (2) peer proof, (3) documentation/transparency, (4) open source as inspectability, (5) benchmarks as screening only, (6) case studies. Case evidence from Cursor, Supabase, Hugging Face, and Stripe confirms: every trust success terminates in developer-verifiable artifacts; every trust failure involves making verification harder. Trust and distribution have reversed in the AI era — trust must precede ecosystem distribution, though individual adoption still precedes individual trust. The behavioral evidence gap (no controlled experiments) is structural but the three-way convergence provides actionable confidence.
