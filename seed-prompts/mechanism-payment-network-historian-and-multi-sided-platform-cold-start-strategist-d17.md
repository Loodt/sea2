## Key Decisions
Visa evolved through five phases, each triggered by a structural failure in the previous governance form: proprietary (1958) to franchise (1966) to member association (1970) to branded scale (1977) to public corporation (2008). Seven specific cold-start mechanisms transfer to the freight NPC: geographic containment, force-enroll one side, unilateral value first, franchise-to-grow with independence before second participant, neutral brand from launch, honor-all-matches rule, and inclusive infrastructure. Six Dee Hock anti-capture governance principles map to NPC Section 21 design, with volume-proportional voting requiring adaptation to prevent large-carrier capture. Three Visa mechanisms do NOT transfer: interchange economics, institutional intermediation, and universal (non-corridor-local) network effects. The NPC's functional substitutes are settlement acceleration, direct trust via blind-switch, and corridor-density effects respectively.
