## Key Decisions
SCN⁻ accumulation in the closed-loop Wits IX-RIL circuit does NOT affect gold leaching kinetics — once formed, SCN⁻ is chemically inert in alkaline cyanide solution and does not consume CN⁻ or block gold surfaces. It DOES reduce resin operating capacity through reversible competitive site occupation, but this effect is attenuated on medium-base gold-selective resins (Purogold MTA9920) which have intrinsically lower SCN selectivity than strong-base resins. The practical SCN⁻ ceiling is 200 mg/L (conservative) to 500 mg/L (aggressive), based on the only published performance data point (MTA9920 functional at 184 mg/L SCN), plant operating practice (50-500 mg/L range before treatment), and the 100-500x gap between circuit concentrations and the 1M KSCN elution threshold. Management via 10-15% solution bleed is sufficient for Wits-level SCN generation (20-50 g/t); biological ASTER treatment is a proven fallback at commercial scale. The remaining gap is a bench test of MTA9920 capacity at 0/100/200/500/1000 mg/L SCN⁻ to quantify the exact capacity reduction curve.
