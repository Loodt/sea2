## Key Decisions
[DERIVED: synthesis of F921,F922,F923] South African law leaves room for a very narrow neutral freight utility, but not for rival-visible live capacity exchange: current or very recent firm-specific corridor signals, especially in concentrated lanes, are high-risk competitively sensitive information. [DERIVED: synthesis of F922,F923] The surviving structure is a blind, rules-based message switch or matching utility run by an independent operator, where each carrier sees only its own invitations and bilateral outcomes, while rival-specific capacity, refusals, customer identity, and trading terms remain hidden. [SOURCE: https://www.compcom.co.za/request-an-advisory-opinion/] There is no general statutory safe harbour for this model, so the practical de-risking path is to design the operating rules tightly first and then seek a section 79A advisory opinion on the disclosed facts. [DERIVED: synthesis of F916,F917,F919,F920,F926,F927] The best analogue is SWIFT or South Africa’s payments-utility pattern at the governance layer, with IATA useful only in modular form such as messaging standards or neutral settlement administration, not as a precedent for member-governed competitor coordination.
