## Key Decisions
The ~70% heavy metal removal rate from activated sludge treatment is NOT directly applicable to gold. No study has measured gold in both WWTP influent and effluent. A mass balance calculation using Westerhoff's 300 ppb sludge gold and typical sludge production (250 mg/L) shows only 0.075 µg Au removed per liter — consistent with 70%+ removal ONLY if influent gold is 0.05-0.15 ppb (typical residential), but implying just 2-10% partitioning at the 0.8-3.2 ppb range cited in Q010. Gold partitioning is speciation-dependent: colloidal Au(0) is removed >90-95% (like other ENPs), but dissolved Au(I)-thiolate complexes likely pass through treatment due to extreme Au-S bond stability (log K 30-36). Best estimate: 40-95% total gold to sludge depending on speciation mix. Prior economic models (F957, F960) assumed 70% and should be flagged as sensitivity-dependent on this parameter.
