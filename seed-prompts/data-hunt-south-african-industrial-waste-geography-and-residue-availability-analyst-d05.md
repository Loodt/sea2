## Key Decisions
All 9 candidate residues have been assessed for dump-scale availability in South Africa. Coal fly ash (~34 Mt/yr, cumulative >500 Mt) and ferrochrome slag (~5+ Mt/yr) are the dominant dump-scale streams, critically co-located in Mpumalanga. BOF/steel slag is available at Vanderbijlpark but Newcastle is closed. GGBFS is commercial, not waste. Phosphogypsum exists at ~35 Mt in Phalaborwa but is geographically isolated. Carbide lime is small-scale but uniquely co-located with Newcastle legacy slag. Red mud and soda-bearing residues are killed — neither exists in SA. Paper sludge ash is marginal. The strongest co-location cluster for blended testing is Mpumalanga (coal ash + FeCr slag), followed by the Vaal Triangle (BOF slag + GGBFS + coal ash) and Newcastle (legacy steel slag + carbide lime).
